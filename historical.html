Hijrah - Migration to Madinah

622 CE, Makkah to Madinah

The Hijrah marks the migration of Prophet Muhammad (PBUH) and his followers from Makkah to Madinah to escape persecution. This event laid the foundation for the first Islamic state.

During this migration, the Muslim community faced many challenges, including hostility from the Quraysh and the hardships of traveling in harsh conditions. The migration demonstrated courage, faith, and trust in Allah.

As a result, the Muslim community grew stronger, establishing Madinah as a center for Islamic governance and education. The Hijrah also marks the beginning of the Islamic calendar.

"And whoever emigrates for the cause of Allah will find on the earth many [alternative] locations and abundance." - Quran 4:100
